What your BMI result means

A BMI range of 18.5 to 24.9 is considered a 'healthy weight'. Maintainig a healthy weight may lower your chances of experiencing health issues later on, such as obesity and type 2 diabetes. Aim for a nutrittious diet with reduced fat and sugar content, incoporating ample fruits and vegetables. Additionally, strive for regular physical activity, ideally about 30 minutes daily for five days a week.

Limitations of BMI

although BMI is often a pratical indicator of healthy weight, it is not suitable for every person, specific group should carefully consider there BMI outcomes, and in certain cases, the measuremnt may not be beneficial to use.

Gender

The development and body fat composition of girls and boys vary with age. consequently, a child's age and gender are considered when evaluating their BMI.

Age

In aging indiduals, increased body fat and muscles loss may cause BMI to underestimate body fat content

Muscle

BMI may misclassify musclar individuals as overweight or obese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat.

Pregnancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to there growing baby. Maintaining a healthy pre-pregnancy BMI is advisable
